     OUR HISTORY.



  In the early part of the 21st century, “paluwagan” which is considered a Filipino tradition of rotating common savings and credit among members of a group, became a common practice among the employees of DSS Productmakers, Inc.

Sometime in January 2004, these employees decided to expand the “paluwagan” practice to a bigger scale and invited more employees that later formed the DSS Employees’ Multi-purpose Cooperative (DEMPC) with the end in view of affording themselves the opportunity to earn additional income from their common savings and credit.

On 3 August 2008, DEMPC entered into Service Agreements with PhilWeb Corporation and BigGame, Inc. for the latter’s manpower requirements which now constitute about eleven (11%) percent of its total manpower pool.

To date, DEMPC’s manpower pool includes beauty consultants, production workers, warehouse helpers, drivers and helpers, merchandisers, office machine operators, and the like. Currently, it has a talent pool of more than 1,800 members and is fully equipped with an efficient computerized payroll system.

In the middle of the year 2004, the DEMPC’s first set of Board of Directors initiated the conversion of the cooperative from a simple common savings and credit association into a full-service manpower provider.
